No Idea How to Upload Your Homework?

Have no fear! By trial and error, I’ve found how to do that for my ENGL488A homework.

  1. Know your student log-in for UMD. (A space on TerpConnect is reserved for every student.)

  2. Open Komodo Edit 8. Go to Edit ► Preferences. A pop-up opens asking for server info. Fill it in like so, using your own Terp username and password.

  3. Open your terpconnect.umd.edu. Click on File Manger in the navigation bar. You’ll see a giant button, Access My Files. Click it. Fill in the blanks of the pop-up with your regular student log-in.

  4. Now you see almost all your server folders. It automatically opens home for you, but you actually need to back up a bit. Click on your student login, and a new list of folders will come up. See pub? That stands for “public access”. That’s where to upload files. Close this. You’re down for now.

  3. To begin uploading your files, you have three choices:

    • Uploading 5 files at a time in TerpConnect, or (proceed to Step 8)
    • Using Firefox FTP or (proceed to Step 9)
    • Saving Remotely from Komodo Edit 8 (proceed to Step 10)

  4. In TerpConnect, you can only upload 5 files at a time. Find your pub folder again, and from the left menu, click upload files. Go through your local folder (on your Desktop, or My Documents, or wherever) and upload all of your .html files and images, as well as the files from the Bootstrap download. Create new folders and label them exactly, css js and img. Sort your files.

  5. Once you’ve downloaded the FireFTP add-on from either the Mozilla website or Dr. Kill’s Springpad link, open Firefox. Click the big, orange Firefox button in your toolbar and select Web Developer. FireFTP opens here.

    Like Komodo Edit 8, you’ll need to set up a hosting server account. Re-key the same information: 

                **Account Name:** TerpConnect
**Host (Name):** `terpconnect.umd.edu`
**Login:** your student name and password       
**(Connection) Security:** SFTP
**Port:** 22

    Now go back to the FireFTP toolbar, and select connect. You may get a message about a registry key in the cache. That’s okay. Proceed. You’ll now be able to copy-paste ENTIRE FOLDERS directly onto the TerpConnect Server. Do so. (Remember to back up from your home screen, and paste everything in pub.)

  6. In Komodo Edit 8, go to File ► Save as Remote. (All this means is that you’re saving your file on a server, not on your own computer space anymore.) Now that your server, TerpConnect, has been set-up, select it from the drop-down menu. All your folders will come up, so just select and save.

    Rinse and repeat for ALL your files.

    You will have to select, cut, and paste all your web files from your home folder into pub, as pub doesn’t automatically appear in Komodo Edit.

    (If Save as Remote is greyed out, make sure your cursor is clicked on the code, not the preview. If you don’t see a folder, hit the Refresh icon.)

  7. To test your page, open a new tab or window in your browser. Type in terpconnect.umd.edu/~name/index.html (with name replace by whatever your username is).

    You should be live now!

    (If your page looks really plain, double-check that all of those other files from Bootstrap were uploaded too. The formatting did not work unless the setup of folders and files was recreated the exact same way of the Bootstrap download. So in your pub folder, create folders, img, css, and js and place your files accordingly. Try again. It should work now.)
